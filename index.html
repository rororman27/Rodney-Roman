Multi-Team Sales Performance Intelligence Platform

The Problem

HR Birthday Label Automation — UKG to Avery Word Labels
🔴
The Problem
Every month, the HR team manually logged into the UKG platform, searched for employees with upcoming birthdays, and hand-typed each person's name and mailing address into a Word document formatted as Avery labels — a process that routinely took several weeks to complete. As the company grew its headcount, the workload scaled with it, pulling HR staff away from higher-value work and creating a recurring bottleneck that had no end in sight.
🔵
What I Built
Built a fully automated end-to-end ETL pipeline that eliminates the manual process entirely. A Python script authenticates with the UKG API and extracts employee records on a scheduled basis, filtering for employees whose birthdays fall in the following calendar month. The relevant fields — full name and mailing address — are transformed and saved into a Delta table in Azure Databricks (mishbricks.ukg.birthdaylabel), giving the company a clean, auditable record of every run. At the end of the pipeline, a Python script generates a properly formatted Microsoft Word document structured to Avery 5160 label specifications (3 columns × 10 rows, 30 labels per page) and delivers it automatically to the HR manager's inbox via email — ready to print, no manual steps required.
🟢
The Impact
A process that previously consumed several weeks of HR staff time each month now completes in minutes and runs without human intervention. The HR team no longer touches the process at all — the label document arrives in their inbox on the first of every month, print-ready. As the company's headcount grows, the pipeline scales with it at zero additional cost in time or effort. What was a recurring administrative burden is now a fully hands-off, reliable workflow.

Field Crew Scheduling & Revenue Tracking Web App
🔴
The Problem
A field services company dispatching blue-collar crews to customer homes was tracking all scheduling and revenue in a shared Google Sheet — one tab per month, branches and crew leaders listed vertically, every day of the month spread horizontally. When a job was assigned, someone manually typed the dollar amount into the cell for that crew leader and day. Multi-day jobs were split by hand (total ÷ days) across each applicable column. With dozens of crews across multiple branches, the sheet became impossible to maintain: entries landed in the wrong rows, split amounts were miscalculated, days were skipped, and no one could tell at a glance what any crew was actually scheduled to do. Month-end revenue reports were inaccurate and took days to reconcile.
🔵
What I Built
Replaced the Google Sheet entirely with a purpose-built scheduling web application using Appsmith as the front-end interface, backed by an Azure App Services API. Dispatchers now select a branch, pick a crew leader, choose a job date range, and enter the contract value — the app automatically distributes the revenue evenly across the scheduled days, writes each entry to a structured database, and displays a live crew calendar so scheduling conflicts are visible before they happen. The interface mirrors the familiar grid layout the team already understood, but with dropdowns, validation, and auto-calculation eliminating every manual step that caused errors.
🟢
The Impact
Data entry time dropped from hours to minutes per day. Revenue figures became accurate in real time — no more end-of-month reconciliation marathons. Because the data was now structured and consistent, it could feed directly into Power BI for branch-level and crew-level performance reporting that simply wasn't possible before. Scheduling conflicts that previously slipped through unnoticed were flagged immediately. The client scaled from 2 branches to 5 without adding any administrative headcount.
