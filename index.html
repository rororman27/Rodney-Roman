⚡ Power BI · DAX · Data Modeling
Multi-Team Sales Performance Intelligence Platform
🔴
The Problem
A regional home services company managing 3 sales teams across the Midwest had no unified view of performance. Sales directors were making compensation and staffing decisions based on gut feel and disconnected Excel files — with no visibility into which teams, advisors, or lead sources were actually driving revenue.
🔵
What I Built
A 4-page interactive Power BI report connecting Sales, Appointments, Leads, and Transactions into a single star-schema model. Built DAX measures for YTD/MTD revenue, close rate, average deal value, and average revenue per appointment — all filterable by team, date range, and lead vendor. Added conditional formatting on the performance table so underperforming teams are flagged instantly in red.
🟢
The Impact
Leadership gained a single source of truth updated daily. The team performance table — with green/yellow/red conditional formatting on Avg $/Deal — eliminated weekly reporting meetings. The vendor quadrant analysis revealed HomeAdvisor delivered 34% of all leads at a 42%+ close rate, informing a reallocation of the lead budget worth $200K+.

📍 Lead & Vendor Analytics
Lead Source ROI & Vendor Quadrant Analysis
🔴
The Problem
The company was spending equally across 5 lead vendors with no data on which ones actually converted to sales — wasting budget on low-performing sources.
🔵
What I Built
A Lead Analysis page featuring a quadrant scatter chart plotting Net Lead volume vs. Lead-to-Sales conversion rate per vendor, with bubble size representing revenue generated. Added a 40.5% average benchmark line and four labeled quadrants — Best Vendors, Scale These, Review ROI, Cut/Renegotiate — so decisions were self-evident.

HomeAdvisor was confirmed as the highest-volume, highest-converting vendor (42%+ close rate). Angi — despite high volume — sat below the conversion benchmark, triggering a contract renegotiation. EverConnect was flagged for scaling despite low volume due to its exceptional conversion efficiency.

HR Birthday Label Automation — UKG to Avery Word Labels
🔴
The Problem
Every month, the HR team manually logged into the UKG platform, searched for employees with upcoming birthdays, and hand-typed each person's name and mailing address into a Word document formatted as Avery labels — a process that routinely took several weeks to complete. As the company grew its headcount, the workload scaled with it, pulling HR staff away from higher-value work and creating a recurring bottleneck that had no end in sight.
🔵
What I Built
Built a fully automated end-to-end ETL pipeline that eliminates the manual process entirely. A Python script authenticates with the UKG API and extracts employee records on a scheduled basis, filtering for employees whose birthdays fall in the following calendar month. The relevant fields — full name and mailing address — are transformed and saved into a Delta table in Azure Databricks (mishbricks.ukg.birthdaylabel), giving the company a clean, auditable record of every run. At the end of the pipeline, a Python script generates a properly formatted Microsoft Word document structured to Avery 5160 label specifications (3 columns × 10 rows, 30 labels per page) and delivers it automatically to the HR manager's inbox via email — ready to print, no manual steps required.
🟢
The Impact
A process that previously consumed several weeks of HR staff time each month now completes in minutes and runs without human intervention. The HR team no longer touches the process at all — the label document arrives in their inbox on the first of every month, print-ready. As the company's headcount grows, the pipeline scales with it at zero additional cost in time or effort. What was a recurring administrative burden is now a fully hands-off, reliable workflow.
